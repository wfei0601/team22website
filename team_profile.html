Executive Summary


Our team started the process of auditing our ability to excel in the MSIS program by reflecting upon our success in the EY Case Competition. We were proud to be selected as a top-five team and earn a spot in the final round. Although we did not make top-three, we valued the experience of presenting in front of the tough final round judges. Overall our team decided upon a rating of six out of ten for this experience because despite our success in placement, we still see opportunities for improvement. The performance evaluation criteria includes the overall outcome of a project, the quality of work contributed, and productivity level. We choose grades and outcome of the project because it is important to all of the team member's performance in the core. We chose quality of work flow because we want all of our team members to contribute its maximum effort to each assignment. Finally, we factor in the time management aspect of the project because we all have a lot of things going on both in MSIS and in our personal lives, and we want to be efficient with our time spent working on projects outside of the classroom. The EY case was a great experience to learn from because we started the process with no knowledge of one another and no experience working together. Our team thought overall the EY case process was helpful to us because it challenged us to spend long hours together and get used to one another. This helped us to become friends and to feel like we had strong connections within the MSIS program already. It also forced us to learn about each other and how each team member makes decisions under pressure. We thought this was important because it made us demonstrate how we all actually work, instead of the normal honeymoon periods groups normally enjoy at first, and it allowed us to see how one another responded to the complexity and ambiguity of the case. That being said, success in the EY case competition did not come without its challenges. One of the first issues we ran into was learning how to deal with each other’s working styles, and how each member of the team responds to criticism. The limited timeframe also made it hard for our team to mesh our ideas together. While we all acknowledge that we each had strong ideas, finding the cohesion that made them flow together during the presentation is something our team struggled with, but think we can figure out in the future.
